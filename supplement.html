About Vitamin A

Vitamin A (VA) need is dependent on the blood concentration of vitamin A, which is present as different molecules, i.e., mainly retinol and provitamin A carotenoids, plus retinyl esters in the postprandial period after a VA-containing meal. VA blood concentration is affected by numerous factors including dietary VA intake, VA absorption efficiency, efficiency of provitamin A carotenoid conversion to VA, VA tissue uptake, etc. Most of these factors are in turn modulated by genetic variations in genes encoding proteins involved in VA metabolism.

Analysing Your Vitamin A Need

Individuals of a population have different absorption and metabolism of vitamin A. Since it is a multipurpose vitamin which is commonly functioning as an antioxidant, for optical nerve health and for bone development, the rate and direction of its metabolism may differ from one person to another. Risk Management & Tests

Prolonged consumtion of large amounts of Vitamin A can result to toxicity because it is a fat soluble vitamin and can be stored primarily in the liver. According to studies, hypervitaminosis A may be caused by abnormal transport and distribution of Vitamin A and its derivatives due to overloading of the plasma transport mechanisms.

Plasma retinol concentrations are useful indicators of healthy or normal Vitamin A status of the body. Make sure to ask professional advise from your doctor before taking any food or dietary supplements to prevent vitamin overdose.
